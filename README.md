# AI Writing Guide

A running reference for making AI-assisted writing sound natural and human. Useful for academic papers, essays, and professional writing.

## Contents

- [Word Swaps](word-swaps.md) — AI-flagged words/phrases and their human alternatives
- [Structural Patterns](structural-patterns.md) — Formatting and structural tells to avoid
- [Punctuation Tells](punctuation-tells.md) — Em dashes, semicolons, and other punctuation giveaways
- [Before & After Examples](before-after-examples.md) — Concrete AI vs human rewrites
- [How Professors Detect AI](professor-detection.md) — What educators actually look for (from Reddit/forums)
- [Detection Deep Dive](detection-info.md) — How AI detectors work and what triggers them
- [Editing Checklist](checklist.md) — Quick pre-submission checklist

## How to Use This

1. Write or generate your draft
2. Run through the [checklist](checklist.md)
3. Use [word swaps](word-swaps.md) to catch obvious AI vocabulary
4. Check [structural patterns](structural-patterns.md) and [punctuation](punctuation-tells.md)
5. Compare against [before & after examples](before-after-examples.md) for inspiration
6. Review [professor detection](professor-detection.md) to know what humans look for
