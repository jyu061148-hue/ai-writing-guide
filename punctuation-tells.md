# Punctuation Tells — The Small Things That Give It Away

## The Em Dash Problem — aka "The ChatGPT Dash"

The single biggest punctuation giveaway. AI models use **8–15 em dashes per 1,000 words** vs **1–2 in human writing**. It's been called "baked into their DNA."

### Why AI overuses em dashes
- Training data includes older books that used them more
- Em dashes are versatile — they replace commas, parentheses, and colons
- Human feedback during training rewarded "snappy" em dash usage
- Even when you prompt AI to stop, it struggles to comply

### How to fix
- **Don't eliminate em dashes entirely** — that looks unnatural too
- Limit to 1–2 per page max
- Replace most with commas, parentheses, or just restructure the sentence
- Ask yourself: would I actually write this with a dash?

**Before:**
> The methodology — which has been widely adopted — provides a framework for analysis — one that addresses both qualitative and quantitative concerns.

**After:**
> The methodology, which has been widely adopted, provides a framework for analysis that addresses both qualitative and quantitative concerns.

## Semicolons: The Missing Tell

AI *avoids* semicolons. Only ~3% of AI-generated text contains them, compared to ~23% of human academic writing. This absence is itself a signal.

### How to fix
- Use semicolons naturally where two related independent clauses connect
- They're especially common in academic writing — their absence stands out

**Before (AI style):**
> The results were significant. They aligned with prior research.

**After (human academic style):**
> The results were significant; they aligned with prior research.

---

## Colons: The "Professionalism" Crutch

AI uses colons as "universal emphasis tools." Every setup gets a colon:

**AI pattern:**
> The finding was clear: social media impacts mental health.
> The implication is straightforward: more research is needed.
> One factor stands out: engagement.

**Fix:** Not every lead-in needs a colon. Sometimes just say it.

---

## Commas: Too Perfect

AI almost never misuses commas. It hits every Oxford comma, every introductory clause comma, every appositive. Real humans are messier — and that messiness is actually a signal of authenticity.

### What to do
- Don't introduce errors on purpose (that's its own red flag)
- But don't over-edit natural writing into AI-level perfection either
- If you used Grammarly's "Rewrite" feature, you may have accidentally AI-ified your punctuation

---

## Overall Pattern

| Punctuation | AI Tendency | Human Tendency |
|---|---|---|
| Em dash (—) | Way overused | Occasional |
| Semicolon (;) | Rarely used | Common in academic writing |
| Colon (:) | Overused for emphasis | More selective |
| Commas | Perfectly placed, always | Sometimes inconsistent |
| Periods | Uniform sentence endings | Varied — fragments, questions mixed in |
| Exclamation marks | Rare in academic, common in casual AI | Varies by context |

---

*This is one of the easier things to fix in a final pass. Do a Ctrl+F for "—" and thin them out.*
