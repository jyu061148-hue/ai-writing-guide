# Pre-Submission Editing Checklist

Run through this before finalizing any AI-assisted writing.

## Word-Level

- [ ] Search for Tier 1 red flag words (delve, moreover, furthermore, leverage, harness, tapestry, etc.)
- [ ] Replace or remove hedging phrases ("it's important to note," "certainly," "arguably")
- [ ] Check for vague hype words (groundbreaking, transformative, innovative) — replace with specifics
- [ ] Add contractions where natural ("don't" not "do not")
- [ ] Remove unnecessary qualifiers ("very," "extremely," "highly")

## Sentence-Level

- [ ] Vary sentence length — mix short and long
- [ ] Check for repeated sentence openers (don't start 3 paragraphs the same way)
- [ ] Convert passive voice to active where possible
- [ ] Read aloud — flag anything that sounds robotic or stiff

## Paragraph-Level

- [ ] Vary paragraph lengths (not all 3-5 sentences)
- [ ] Check that transitions are natural, not mechanical ("Moreover," "Furthermore")
- [ ] Remove excessive signposting ("Let's explore," "As mentioned")

## Structure-Level

- [ ] Opening isn't template-y ("This paper will discuss...")
- [ ] Conclusion adds something new (not just restating the intro)
- [ ] No unnecessary bullet lists in essay-style writing
- [ ] Points get unequal weight — not everything neatly symmetrical

## Content-Level

- [ ] Specific examples, names, numbers (not just generalizations)
- [ ] Your actual opinion/analysis is present (not just neutral summary)
- [ ] Personal voice comes through — would someone recognize this as *your* writing?
- [ ] All sources/references are real (AI can hallucinate citations)

## Final Pass

- [ ] Read the whole thing aloud one more time
- [ ] If allowed, run through GPTZero or similar as a sanity check
- [ ] Save your drafts/notes as evidence of your writing process

---

*Customize this for your specific use case. Not every item applies to every type of writing.*
